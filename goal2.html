Goal 2

Understand the essential nature of information and its relevance to society.

Outcome 2B: Explain the impact of information policies on intellectual freedom, access, literacy, information behavior, and other aspects of library and information science.

Discussion post on the web's complex social and legal issues for LIS 753: Internet Fundamentals and Design.

This artifact analyzes the discussion surrounding the web and the efforts to revise copyright laws and intellectual properties. Through this post, I view the debate through a library and information professional lens and how we, as librarians, teach our patrons about this issue. This discussion post also focuses on the impact of broadband access patrons have outside of the library and the library's role in providing it. I also discuss that librarians and information professionals should be aware of and knowledgeable of information policies locally and globally.

Outcome 2C: Assess the information needs and interests of diverse communities and organizations.

Physical Access discussion post for LIS 710: Information Divides.

For this artifact, I chose the physical discussion post that looks at two socially and financially diverse populations and assesses their needs and interests regarding physical access to digital media. Through this comparison, I articulated their differences and how it impacts the families, particularly the school-age children, who were trying to juggle online school with limited digital media access. In the discussion post, I assess the families' needs and note how these social and financial differences affect their ability to participate in an information and technology-driven society.

Outcome 2D: Develop appropriate responses to information needs.

Grant Proposal for LIS 725: Curriculum and School Libraries.

I created a grant proposal for a K-8th public school in California based on the needs of the 1st through 3rd-grade classes in providing headphones for the academic school year of 2020-2021. I chose the need for headphones for each student due to the COVID-19 pandemic and students needing personal headphones to ensure their health and safety. After talking with a school teacher about what the information needs are in her class, I created the proposal for the upcoming school year. This grant proposal allowed students to receive headphones and created a program called "We Listen" to work in tandem with the already existing program I Read that the teachers utilize.
