Goal 4

Synthesize theory and practice within a dynamic and evolving information environment.

Outcome 4A: Apply library and information science theories, principles, and research to professional practice.

School Libraries and Reluctant Readers Research Paper for LIS 701: Core Values, Ethics and Issues in Library and Information Profession.

This research paper focuses on the question of "How do I move my student from a reluctant reader to an enthusiastic reader?" Throughout the artifact, I look at the current theories, principles, research, and practices in the library and schools to provide practical methods for librarians, teachers, and family members in helping their reluctant readers. I include current methods used in both library and classroom settings while analyzing the various data on their pros and cons and add alternative ways one could use instead of or in tandem.

Outcome 4B: Descrive how library and information science theories and practices may inform and improve emerging technologies.

Library Service Evaluation Paper for LIS 708: Evidence Based Planning, Management, and Decision Making.

Library and Information science theories and practices revolve around the desire to provide information access to their patrons. As such, libraries have continued to pivot and make changes with the increase and introduction of technologies. Through this artifact, I focus on how those theories and practices currently in place can inform and improve emerging technologies, particularly the library's application of social media. Social media is a technology that is constantly emerging and evolving in new ways. As such, I focus on how a public library can implement the two most popular sites with teens, Instagram and TikTok, to reach their teen patrons. Through the paper, I argue that the library already has the theories and practices but can better inform and improve their use in these technologies and better impact their relationship with the teen population.

Outcome 4C: Demonstrate teaching and learning principles in relation to professional practice.

Digital Citizenship Lesson Plan for LIS 725: Curriculum and School Libraries.

This artifact is a lesson plan that focuses on primary versus secondary sources taught within a school library to an 8th-grade class. The lesson includes: the learning outcomes and objects, the Illinois Learning Standards, and a step-by-step guide on how the teaching portion would occur in a professional setting. From this artifact, one can see that I meet the outcome of 4C in demonstrating both teaching and learning principles in a school library setting. I provide examples in my lesson plan on expected questions and answers that students will give and ask throughout the lesson. While also including how I will respond and make changes to my class based on students' reactions, questions, answers, and feedback.
