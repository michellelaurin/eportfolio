Goal 3

Navigate, curate and create information across the spectrum of human records.

Outcome 3A: Design resources or tools that facilitate access to information.

Digital Resource Bibliography for LIS 786: Advanced Web Design and LIS 815: History of Folk and Fairy Tales for Youth.

For this artifact, I created a website titled: The World Of Folk And Fairy Tales. The website contains information about folk and fairy tales, their definitions, and their history. It also has two pages that provide stories found worldwide and annotated bibliographies on folk and fairy tale books and collections. These two web pages allow users to read some tales in their entirety and find their source on Project Gutenberg and find out what people group and nation these tales originate in the version provided. On the annotated bibliographies page, I created a list of book resources people could learn about that are also organized by location or by the author's last name. This page gives the user access to information about the book before they pick up the book.

Outcome 3C: Evaluate resources or tools that facilitate access to and manage information.

Context Book Paper for LIS 718: Storytelling for Adults and Children.

I evaluated the book, The Storyteller's Start-Up Book: Finding, Learning, Performing and Using Folktales by Margaret Read MacDonald. The evaluation of this resource allows beginning storytellers to decide whether this is a tool that they would like to learn from and possibly include in their collection. Through this evaluation, I focus on the book's contents, its resources, tale choices, and included activities. The paper focuses on the book's overall usefulness and the author's authority on the subject of storytelling. This artifact accomplishes outcome 3C by evaluating a resource that facilitates access to and manages helpful information to a storyteller.

Outcome 3D: Assess applicability of current and emergy technologies to information management.

Hands-On Research Paper for LIS 708: Evidence Based Planning, Management, and Decision Making.

This artifact focuses on electronic textbooks increase in the academic sphere and whether it is a technology that current MLIS students use for their information management. Through the research, the group analyzed electronic textbook trends for other academic studies and interviewed current MLIS students on their textbook choices. This artifact meets this outcome by looking at the surrounding factors of the current technologies and emerging ones that provide electronic textbooks for students. It focuses on how students create and manage their collection of books that offer resources and information for their studies. The research found that although e-textbooks were applicable in some ways, there was still a need for both current and emerging technology to be fully accessible for all users.
